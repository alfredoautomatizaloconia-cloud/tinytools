How to install

Unzip into your site's /public or /static folder, then paste this into your <head>:

<link rel="icon" type="image/png" sizes="32x32" href="/favicon-32x32.png">
<link rel="icon" type="image/png" sizes="16x16" href="/favicon-16x16.png">
<link rel="apple-touch-icon" sizes="180x180" href="/apple-touch-icon.png">
<link rel="manifest" href="/manifest.json">

Pro tips for a great favicon

  • 16×16 is everything. Most users see your favicon at this tiny size in a tab. Test it. If it's unreadable, simplify.
  • Avoid thin lines. They disappear at small sizes. Bold shapes win.
  • One letter or one symbol usually beats a logo squashed into 16 pixels.
  • High contrast with both light and dark browser themes — test both.

FAQ

Why no .ico file?

Modern browsers (since 2015) accept .png as favicon. Skipping .ico keeps the kit smaller and avoids legacy-format compatibility issues. If you specifically need .ico for old IE compatibility, rename favicon-32x32.png to favicon.ico — most servers serve it correctly.

Is the manifest.json the right one for my framework?

The bundled manifest follows the W3C spec and works as-is for static sites, Vercel, Netlify, GitHub Pages. Frameworks like Next.js or Vite auto-detect it from /public.
